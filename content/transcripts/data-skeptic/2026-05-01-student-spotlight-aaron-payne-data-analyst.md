## Transcript

In this episode, Kyle Polich interviews Aaron Payne, an MBA student at Georgia Tech (business analytics) and a Senior Insights Analyst at Chick-fil-A. Aaron shares how a career path that began in technical tax transformation and Python scripting led to analytics roles across industries, and why the most important part of analytics is not the model, but the insight that drives an action.

Aaron walks through an experiential learning practicum project with Comfama, a social services organization in Colombia, where the team built a forecasting approach to better estimate demand for services amid economic volatility. They discuss what “data cleaning” looks like when the dataset is in another language, how they balanced interpretability with performance, and why they combined a seasonal model with exogenous variables and a machine learning ensemble to improve results, including how they handled COVID-era disruption in the data.
